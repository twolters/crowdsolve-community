## **Lesson 1 \- Identify the top 3 problems and current solutions**

## **Goal**

Break your refined problem into the **three most important problems** your early customer experiences.

Most startups fail because they try to solve too many problems at once.  
This exercise forces you to focus on what matters most.

---

## **Exercise**

Start with your **Refined Problem Statement** from Stage 1\.

Now identify the **three most important problems** your customer faces.

For each problem, answer:

1️⃣ What is the problem?  
2️⃣ How does the customer currently deal with it?

---

## **Guidance**

When describing problems:

• Be specific and concrete  
• Describe real-world situations  
• Avoid abstract language

When describing current solutions:

Think about what customers actually do today:

• software tools  
• manual processes  
• hiring people  
• workarounds  
• ignoring the problem

👉 If customers are already spending **time or money**, that’s a strong signal of real pain.

---

## **Example**

Problem 1  
Small e-commerce brands frequently ship orders late during peak demand.

Current Solution  
They manually track orders in spreadsheets and try to prioritize fulfillment.

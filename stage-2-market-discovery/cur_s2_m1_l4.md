# Lesson 4 \- Map the solution hypothesis

## **Goal**

Connect your solution directly to the problems you identified.

This ensures your idea is grounded in **real customer pain**, not abstract thinking.

---

## **Exercise**

For each problem from Lesson 1, describe:

👉 How your solution would address it

---

## **Template**

Problem 1  
Solution hypothesis as a feature of your product or service

Problem 2  
Solution hypothesis as a feature of your product or service

Problem 3  
Solution hypothesis as a feature of your product or service

---

## **Guidance**

Describe solutions in **simple, conceptual terms**, not detailed features.

Examples:

• A tool that automates X  
• A service that helps with Y  
• A platform that connects A and B

Focus on:

• what changes for the customer  
• how the problem gets solved

---

## **Example**

Problem  
Manual order tracking causes delays

Solution Hypothesis  
A system that automatically prioritizes and tracks fulfillment tasks in real time

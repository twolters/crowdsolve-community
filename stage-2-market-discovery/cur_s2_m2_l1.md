# Module overview and Lesson 1 \- Identify Interview Candidates

## **Module Overview**

You now have:

• a defined customer  
• a clear problem  
• a solution hypothesis

These are still **assumptions**.

In this module, you will step out of your head and into the real world.

👉 You will talk to real people and learn how they actually behave.

---

## **What This Module Is (and Isn’t)**

This is NOT about:

• pitching your idea  
• getting validation  
• convincing people

This IS about:

• learning how customers behave  
• understanding real problems  
• identifying patterns across conversations

---

## **Key Principle**

People don’t tell you the truth about ideas.  
They DO tell you the truth about their past behavior.

---

## **Lesson 1 — Identify Interview Candidates**

### **Goal**

Find real people who match your **early adopter customer segment**.

Talking to the wrong people will give you misleading signals.

---

### **Exercise — Find Your First 10–20 People**

Using your customer definition from Module 2.1, answer:

👉 Where do these people exist?

---

### **Possible Sources**

• LinkedIn (search by role, company, industry)  
• Slack communities or Discord groups  
• Industry forums or niche communities  
• Professional associations  
• Personal network  
• Events (virtual or in-person)  
• Cold outreach

---

### **Rules**

• Be specific — “Operations Managers at logistics companies,” not “business owners”  
• Choose places where you can realistically reach people  
• Prioritize people you can contact **this week**

---

### **Outreach Message Template**

Keep it simple and honest and loosely use the following format. Remember these work better the more they connect:

Hi \[Name\],  
I’m researching how \[customer type\] deal with \[problem\].  
I’m not selling anything — just hoping to learn from people with experience.  
Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation?

## **Lean CRM for Customer Discovery (10–20 Candidates)**

### **Core Principle**

Keep it simple. You’re not building a sales pipeline—you’re building a **learning pipeline**. Use the outline below to build your own Lean CRM spreadsheet. Or, use a tool like Attio or Streak.

---

### **Recommended Columns**

**1\. Contact Information**

• Name  
• Company  
• Role / Title  
• LinkedIn / Profile Link  
• Email / Contact Method

---

**2\. Customer Fit**

• Customer Segment  
(From your Lean Canvas — helps validate targeting)

• Fit Score (1–5)  
How well they match your ideal early adopter

---

**3\. Source**

• Source  
(LinkedIn, referral, community, etc.)

• Notes on Source  
(Optional — e.g., “Intro from Sarah”)

---

**4\. Outreach Tracking**

• Outreach Status  
Dropdown:

* Not Contacted  
* Contacted  
* Replied  
* Scheduled  
* Completed  
* No Response

• Date Contacted  
• Follow-Up Date

---

**5\. Interview Tracking**

• Interview Scheduled (Y/N)  
• Interview Date  
• Interview Completed (Y/N)

---

**6\. Key Insights Capture (Lightweight)**

• Problem Observed (1 line)  
What problem did they describe?

• Current Solution  
What are they doing today?

• Pain Level (1–5)  
Your subjective rating

• Notable Quote  
Exact language (VERY valuable later)

---

**7\. Signal Strength**

• Signal Strength (1–5)  
How strong was the validation?

Guide:  
1 \= weak / unclear  
3 \= moderate  
5 \= strong pain \+ clear behavior

---

**8\. Follow-Up / Opportunity**

• Interested in Follow-Up? (Y/N)  
• Potential Early User? (Y/N)  
• Notes / Observations

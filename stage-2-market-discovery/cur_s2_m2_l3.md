# Lesson 3 \- conduct interviews

## **Goal**

Run conversations that generate **real insights**, not surface-level feedback.

---

## **Interview Structure (15–20 minutes)**

### **1\. Introduction**

Briefly explain:

“I’m researching how \[customer type\] deal with \[problem\]. I’m not selling anything.”

---

### **2\. Understand Context**

Ask about:

• their role  
• their responsibilities  
• their workflow

---

### **3\. Explore the Problem**

Ask:

• when the problem happens  
• what triggers it  
• how often it occurs

For this section, you should be using your interview questions prepared in the previous module for this. Go ahead and modify them if you feel you need to.

---

### **4\. Current Solutions**

Understand:

• what they do today  
• what tools they use  
• what workarounds exist

---

### **5\. Pain & Consequences**

Explore:

• what happens when things go wrong  
• time lost  
• money lost  
• frustration

---

## **Critical Rule**

👉 Do NOT pitch your solution.

If they ask, keep it vague or redirect:

“I’m still exploring — I’d love to understand your experience first.”

---

## **What to Capture**

Take notes on:

• specific problems mentioned  
• exact language they use  
• current tools and behaviors  
• emotional signals (frustration, stress, urgency)

---

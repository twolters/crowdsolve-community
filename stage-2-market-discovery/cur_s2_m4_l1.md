## **Goal**

Get **early, high-quality feedback** on your landing page from people who already understand the problem.

Before testing at scale, you want to answer:

**“Is this message clear and compelling to the people who actually experience the problem?”**

---

## **Why This Matters**

At this stage, your biggest risk is not lack of traffic.

It’s:

• unclear messaging  
• weak problem articulation  
• confusing value proposition

The fastest way to improve is to:

👉 go back to people you’ve already spoken with

These are your **highest-signal reviewers**.

---

## **Key Principle**

Don’t ask strangers first.  
👉 Ask people who already understand the problem.

---

## **Exercise — Send Your Landing Page for Feedback**

Go back to:

• people you interviewed  
• strong candidates from your outreach list  
• anyone who clearly experienced the problem

---

## **Outreach Message Template**

Keep it simple and direct:

“Hey \[Name\],  
I took what you shared with me and put together a simple page describing the idea.  
Would you mind taking a quick look and telling me if this feels accurate and compelling?  
No pressure — just looking for honest feedback.”

Include your landing page link

---

## **What You’re Trying to Learn**

Ask for feedback on:

---

### **1\. Clarity**

Do they understand:

• the problem  
• who it’s for  
• what it’s trying to do

---

### **2\. Relevance**

Do they feel:

👉 “This is describing my situation”

---

### **3\. Value**

Do they care about the outcome?

👉 “Would this actually help me?”

---

### **4\. Action**

Would they:

• sign up  
• want to learn more  
• ignore it

---

## **Optional Follow-Up Questions**

If they respond, ask:

• “What part resonated most?”  
• “What felt unclear or confusing?”  
• “Would you personally sign up? Why or why not?”  
• “What would make this more compelling?”

---

## **What to Capture**

For each person:

• Did they respond?  
• What feedback did they give?  
• Did they express interest?  
• Would they take action?

Capture exact phrases when possible

---

## **⚠️ Important**

Don’t defend your idea.

Don’t explain too much.

👉 Let the landing page speak for itself.

---

## **How to Interpret Feedback**

Look for patterns:

• Where people get confused  
• What they repeat back to you  
• What they *don’t* mention  
• Whether they would take action

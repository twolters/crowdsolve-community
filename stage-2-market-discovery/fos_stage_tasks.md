Here’s a **practical, execution-focused 12-week (3 month) founder to-do plan** aligned with:

• Stage 2 — Market Discovery
• Your cohort cadence
• The Founder Operating System (learn → do → reflect loop)

This is designed so founders always know:

👉 *“What should I be doing this week?”*

---

# 🚀 CrowdSolve Stage 2 — Founder To-Do Plan (12 Weeks)

---

# 🟢 Week 1 — Reground + Setup

## Focus: Get ready to learn fast

### To-Do

• Refine **Problem Statement**
• Refine **Solution Hypothesis**
• Set up your **Founder OS stack**:

* CRM (Google Sheet)
* Interview doc
* Lean Canvas doc

• Identify:

* your **target customer**
* where to find them

---

### Output

✅ Clear problem + solution
✅ System set up

---

# 🟢 Week 2 — Lock Hypotheses

## Focus: Clarity before action

### To-Do

• Finalize:

* Top 3 problems
* Customer segment
* Value proposition

• Pressure test:

* “Is this specific enough?”
* “Could I explain this in 1 sentence?”

---

### Output

✅ Clean Lean Canvas (v1)
👉 Ready to talk to customers

---

# 🟡 Week 3 — Start Outreach

## Focus: Build your interview pipeline

### To-Do

• Identify 15–25 potential interview candidates
• Start outreach (goal: 10+ messages)

• Draft interview questions (Mom Test)

---

### Weekly Targets

• 10–20 outreach attempts
• 3–5 responses

---

### Output

✅ Interview pipeline started
✅ Questions ready

---

# 🟡 Week 4 — First Interviews

## Focus: Start learning from reality

### To-Do

• Conduct 3–5 interviews
• Capture notes immediately after each

• Track:

* problems
* behaviors
* tools used
* pain

---

### Output

✅ First real customer insights

---

# 🔵 Week 5 — Build Interview Momentum

## Focus: Increase volume + confidence

### To-Do

• Conduct 3–5 more interviews
• Continue outreach to fill pipeline

• Start noticing:

* repeated problems
* repeated language

---

### Output

✅ 6–10 interviews completed
👉 Patterns starting to emerge

---

# 🔵 Week 6 — Pattern Recognition

## Focus: Turn conversations into insights

### To-Do

• Review all interview notes
• Identify:

* problem patterns
* common workarounds
* customer language

• Submit to Marvin for synthesis

---

### Output

✅ Customer Insights Report
✅ Updated Lean Canvas

---

# 🟣 Week 7 — Competitive Landscape

## Focus: Understand the real market

### To-Do

• Identify 5–10 competitors:

* direct
* substitutes
* adjacent

• Build competitor table

---

### Output

✅ Market landscape mapped

---

# 🟣 Week 8 — Market Size (TAM / SAM / SOM)

## Focus: Is this worth pursuing?

### To-Do

• Estimate:

* TAM
* SAM
* SOM

• Document:

* assumptions
* logic

---

### Output

✅ Market size model (even if rough)

---

# 🟠 Week 9 — Landing Page Creation

## Focus: Turn insights into messaging

### To-Do

• Create simple landing page:

* problem
* value proposition
* solution concept
* CTA

• Use Marvin if needed

---

### Output

✅ Landing page ready

---

# 🟠 Week 10 — Early Feedback Loop

## Focus: Refine messaging before scale

### To-Do

• Send landing page to:

* 5–10 interviewees

• Collect feedback:

* clarity
* relevance
* interest

• Refine messaging

---

### Output

✅ Improved messaging
✅ Early signal of interest

---

# 🔴 Week 11 — Public Demand Test

## Focus: Real-world signal

### To-Do

• Post on:

* LinkedIn OR
* Reddit / community

• Promote:

* DM 5–10 people
* engage with responses

---

### Track

• Impressions
• Engagement
• Clicks
• Signups

---

### Output

✅ Demand signal data

---

# 🔴 Week 12 — Analyze + Decide

## Focus: Make a go-forward decision

### To-Do

• Analyze funnel:

* where drop-off occurs

• Reflect:

* what did you learn?
* what changed?

• Decide:

👉 Move forward with MVP
👉 Refine idea
👉 Pivot

---

### Output

✅ Clear next step

---

# 🔁 Weekly Operating System (Always Running)

Every week:

---

## 🎯 Core Actions

• 10–20 outreach attempts
• 3–5 interviews OR experiments
• Capture insights immediately
• Identify 1 pattern

---

## 🧠 Reflection

Ask:

• What did I learn?
• What surprised me?
• What changed?

---

# 🔥 Success by End of 12 Weeks

Founders should have:

• clear customer understanding
• validated (or invalidated) problem
• refined solution hypothesis
• real market context
• early demand signals

👉 AND most importantly:

**confidence in what to do next**

# Evaluation Instructions

Evaluate the founder’s questions for:

---

## 1. Behavioral Focus

Do the questions:
👉 ask about real past behavior?

Strong questions:

* ask about recent events
* ask about workflows
* ask about actions already taken

Weak questions:

* ask hypotheticals
* ask future intent
* ask opinions

---

## 2. Problem Discovery Strength

Do the questions:
👉 uncover real customer pain?

Strong questions:

* reveal operational friction
* uncover consequences
* surface recurring pain

Weak questions:

* stay superficial
* fail to uncover stakes

---

## 3. Current Solution Discovery

Do the questions:
👉 reveal what customers already do today?

Strong questions:

* uncover tools
* uncover workarounds
* uncover manual processes
* reveal spending or effort

Weak questions:

* skip current behavior
* assume no solutions exist

---

## 4. Non-Leading Structure

Do the questions:
👉 avoid biasing the customer?

Strong questions:

* feel open-ended
* avoid suggesting answers
* avoid implying the startup is correct

Weak questions:

* lead the customer
* ask for validation
* contain product assumptions

---

## 5. Simplicity & Natural Conversation Flow

Could these questions:
👉 work in a real 15-minute conversation?

Strong interview guides:

* feel conversational
* flow naturally
* encourage follow-up discussion

Weak guides:

* sound robotic
* overly formal
* too long or complicated

---

## 6. Alignment With Customer Segment

Do the questions:
👉 align with:

* the target customer
* the customer workflow
* the identified problems

Strong alignment:

* feels highly relevant to the customer’s world

Weak alignment:

* feels generic
* disconnected from earlier lessons

# 🧩 Refined Interview Guide

Create a short interview guide using the founder’s questions.

The guide should include:

---

## Interview Introduction

A short conversational opening that:

* reduces pressure
* clarifies learning intent
* avoids pitching

Example tone:

> “I’m researching how people currently handle [problem area]. I’m not selling anything — I’m just trying to learn how this works in the real world.”

---

## Core Interview Questions

Provide a refined version of the founder’s:

* 5–7 interview questions

The revised questions should:

* sound natural
* encourage storytelling
* uncover workflows
* reveal emotional signals
* identify current behavior

---

## Suggested Follow-Up Prompts

Provide:
5–8 lightweight follow-up prompts such as:

* “Can you walk me through that?”
* “What happened next?”
* “How often does this happen?”
* “What’s frustrating about that?”
* “How are you handling that today?”
* “How much time does that take?”
* “Who else is involved?”

These should help founders:
👉 go deeper without leading the customer.

---

## 🚨 Questions to Avoid

Identify any founder questions that:

* seek validation
* ask hypotheticals
* unintentionally pitch the idea

Explain:
👉 why they are risky.

---

## ⭐ Interview Guide Score (1–5)

Evaluate the overall quality of the founder’s interview guide.

### Scoring Rubric

⭐ 1 — Highly leading or validation-seeking
⭐ 2 — Some useful questions, but weak discovery structure
⭐ 3 — Good enough to begin interviews
⭐ 4 — Strong behavioral interview framework
⭐ 5 — Excellent discovery-oriented interview guide

---

## 📊 Score Explanation

Explain:
👉 why this score was given

Focus on:

* behavioral focus
* learning orientation
* pain discovery
* conversational quality
* avoidance of bias

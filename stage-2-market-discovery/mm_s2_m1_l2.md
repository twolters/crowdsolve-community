# Evaluation Instructions

Evaluate the founder’s work for:

---

## 1. Customer Specificity

Can you clearly picture:
👉 who this customer is?

Strong customer definitions:

* identify a specific role
* imply context and workflow
* feel reachable

Weak definitions:

* are broad
* abstract
* demographic-heavy
* impossible to target directly

---

## 2. End User Clarity

Is it clear:
👉 who actually experiences the pain day-to-day?

Strong answers:

* identify operational pain owners
* imply direct workflow interaction

Weak answers:

* confuse executives with operators
* ignore actual users

---

## 3. Economic Buyer Clarity

Is it clear:
👉 who controls purchasing decisions?

Strong answers:

* identify budget owners
* identify decision-makers
* reflect realistic buying behavior

Weak answers:

* assume users automatically buy
* ignore organizational dynamics

---

## 4. Reachability

Could the founder:
👉 realistically talk to 10–20 of these people soon?

Strong segments:

* exist in identifiable communities
* exist on LinkedIn/Slack/Reddit
* are professionally reachable

Weak segments:

* are too diffuse
* impossible to access early

---

## 5. Problem ↔ Customer Alignment

Do the customer definitions:
👉 align with:

* the top 3 problems
* current workarounds
* jobs-to-be-done
* solution hypothesis

Strong alignment:

* creates a believable operational story

Weak alignment:

* creates mismatched customer logic

---

## 6. Early Adopter Focus

Did the founder:
👉 narrow toward a likely early adopter?

Strong early adopters:

* feel pain intensely
* already seek workarounds
* likely care enough to engage

Weak targeting:

* attempts to serve broad markets too early

## Lesson 3 — Public Demand Test (LinkedIn or Communities)

---

### Goal

Test your idea in a real environment and measure:

👉 **Do people engage?**  
👉 **Do they click?**  
👉 **Do they sign up?**

This is your first **public demand signal test**.

---

### Why This Matters

In Lesson 2, you tested messaging with people who already understand the problem.

Now you’re testing:

👉 **Can your message attract and convert people at the top of the funnel?**

---

### Key Principle

If the right people see your idea…  
👉 do they care enough to take action?

---

Step 1 — Choose the Right Channel

Not every idea belongs on LinkedIn.

Choose where your **customer actually exists**.

---

### 🟦 Option 1 — LinkedIn

Use LinkedIn if your customer is:

• professionals (operators, managers, founders, etc.)  
• in B2B environments  
• active in industry conversations

---

### 🟥 Option 2 — Reddit (Subreddits)

Use Reddit if your customer:

• participates in niche communities  
• discusses problems openly  
• prefers anonymity or candid discussion

---

### 🟨 Option 3 — Communities

Examples:

• Slack groups  
• Discord servers  
• Industry forums

Use these if:

• your ICP is active there  
• discussions are problem-focused  
• you can engage authentically

---

### How to Decide

Ask:

👉 “Where would my customer naturally talk about this problem?”

That’s where you go.

---

Step 2 — What Makes a Strong Post

You are NOT pitching.

You are:

👉 sharing insight \+ inviting response

---

### Structure of a High-Performing Post

#### 1\. Hook (First 1–2 Lines)

Make it relatable:

• a problem  
• a pattern  
• a surprising insight

Example:

“I’ve been talking to \[customer type\], and I keep hearing the same issue…”

---

#### 2\. Insight (From Interviews)

Show you’ve done the work:

“Most of them are currently doing \[workaround\], which leads to \[pain\]”

---

#### 3\. The Idea (Light Touch)

Introduce your concept:

“I’m exploring a simple way to solve this…”

---

#### 4\. Call to Action (CTA)

Invite action:

• “Curious if this resonates?”  
• “Would love your thoughts”  
• “If this is something you deal with, I put together a quick page”

👉 Include your landing page link

---

LinkedInTips

### Avoid

• sounding like a pitch  
• listing features  
• being overly polished or “salesy”

👉 Authentic \> polished

---

Step 3 — Promote the Post (Critical)

Posting alone is not enough.

You need to **drive initial engagement**.

---

### On LinkedIn

**Target the ICP directly on LinkedIn**

* **Call Out the ICP:** State the job title or industry in the first sentence of your post (e.g., "If you are a Head of Sales at a Series A SaaS...").  
* **Define the Problem:** Highlight a highly specific, costly problem your ICP faces daily instead of listing your startup's generic software features.  
* **Present the Solution:** Share a concrete framework, checklist, or case study demonstrating how your startup eliminates that exact issue.  
* **Keep Links Out:** Place links to your startup website in the comments section or featured profile section because the algorithm limits the reach of posts containing external links.

After posting:

DM 5–10 relevant people:

“Hey, I just posted something based on conversations I’ve been having — would love your take.”

• Reply quickly to comments  
• Engage with anyone who interacts

---

### In Communities / Reddit

• Follow community rules  
• Frame as a discussion, not promotion  
• Ask for input

Example:

“Curious how others deal with this — I’ve been seeing \[pattern\]”

---

Step 4 — Measure Demand Signals

---

### Funnel to Track

Use the analytics available on the platform to track the following 4 things\!

---

### **1\. Impressions (Top of Funnel)**

How many people saw your post?

👉 Signal: Is the topic interesting?

---

### **2\. Engagement**

• Comments  
• Messages  
• Reactions (secondary)

👉 Signal: Do people relate?

---

### **3\. Clicks to Landing Page**

How many people clicked your link?

👉 Signal: Is your idea compelling enough to explore?

---

### **4\. Conversions (Waitlist Signups)**

How many people signed up?

👉 Signal: Do they care enough to act?

---

### What Good Looks Like (Early Stage)

Even small numbers matter:

• 10–50 clicks  
• 2–10 signups  
• meaningful comments or DMs

# Lesson 5 \- The assumption stack

*Every solution hypothesis rests on hidden assumptions.*

### **Create the following list:**

1. Customer behavior assumption  
   (Will they actually use it?)  
2. Value assumption  
   (Will it meaningfully improve their situation?)  
3. Feasibility assumption  
   (Can this technically work?)  
4. Willingness-to-pay assumption  
   (Will they pay or invest time?)

### **Which assumption is the riskiest?**

That likely becomes your first MVP test.

### **Shopify Example**

*We believe that first-time Shopify store owners doing $5k–$20k/month who manually manage fulfillment will reduce late shipments if we provide automated fulfillment workflow tracking.*

**Customer Behavior Assumption**

* **Risk Level:** High  
* **Core Risk:** Shopify store owners may continue manually managing fulfillment because they are used to spreadsheets and existing workflows.  
* **How We Test:**  
  * Conduct 10 customer interviews  
  * Ask founders to walk through their current fulfillment process  
  * Offer a lightweight prototype or workflow demo to test willingness to try a new system  
* **Success Signal:**  
  * At least 6 out of 10 interviewees say they would actively try a simpler workflow tool  
  * Customers agree to a follow-up demo or early access conversation

---

**Value Assumption**

* **Risk Level:** High  
* **Core Risk:** Reducing manual tracking may not actually solve the founder’s biggest operational pain.  
* **How We Test:**  
  * Ask interviewees to rank their fulfillment frustrations  
  * Ask how much time, stress, or revenue impact late shipments create  
* **Success Signal:**  
  * Multiple founders identify fulfillment coordination as a top operational pain  
  * Interviewees describe measurable frustration, delays, or customer impact

---

**Feasibility Assumption**

* **Risk Level:** Medium  
* **Core Risk:** Automatically consolidating fulfillment workflows across multiple tools and systems may be technically difficult or unreliable.  
* **How We Test:**  
  * Build a lightweight prototype integrating Shopify \+ ShipStation  
  * Test whether fulfillment status can be automatically tracked accurately  
* **Success Signal:**  
  * Prototype successfully pulls accurate fulfillment data  
  * Minimal manual intervention required

---

**Willingness-to-Pay Assumption**

* **Risk Level:** Medium  
* **Core Risk:** Store owners may acknowledge the pain but still refuse to pay because manual workflows feel “good enough.”  
* **How We Test:**  
  * Present mock pricing during interviews  
  * Run a landing page with a waitlist CTA  
  * Measure demo requests and signup interest  
* **Success Signal:**  
  * Prospects join the waitlist  
  * Customers request demos or early access  
  * Interviewees indicate willingness to pay monthly for the solution

---

🚨 Riskiest Assumption

**Customer Behavior Assumption**

This is likely the riskiest assumption early on.

Why?

Even if the pain is real, founders may:

* resist changing workflows  
* continue using spreadsheets out of habit  
* prefer “duct tape” operational processes  
* avoid onboarding another tool

This means the first MVP test should probably focus on:

👉 “Will customers actually change behavior and try a new workflow?”

—not building a full product immediately.

---

💡 Key Learning for Founders

A startup usually fails first because:

* customers don’t care enough,  
* or they won’t change behavior,

—not because the technology couldn’t eventually be built.

# Lesson 1 \- Act 1: The Problem (The Villain)

### **By this point in the Ideate stage you have done the hardest work of early entrepreneurship:**

* You have defined a **clear problem statement**.  
* You have formed a **solution hypothesis**.  
* You understand **who your early customer might be**.  
* You have reflected on your **strengths and gaps as a founder**.

Now you need to learn how to **tell the story of your startup clearly and compellingly.**

Being able to communicate your idea is critical for:

* Recruiting cofounders  
* Attracting mentors  
* Getting customer interviews  
* Raising early funding  
* Building momentum

In this module you will develop a **startup narrative** using a simple storytelling structure used by many founders and investors.

The narrative has **three acts**:

**Act 1 — The Problem (The Villain)**  
Help people feel the pain your customer experiences.

**Act 2 — The Solution (The Hero)**  
Introduce your idea as a way to solve that problem.

**Act 3 — The Future (The Vision)**  
Explain why this idea matters and why you are the person to pursue it.

By the end of this module you will have a **clear startup story** that can later be turned into a pitch deck.

## **Act 1: The Problem (The Villain)**

### **Goal**

Create a clear and compelling description of the problem your startup is trying to solve.

The goal is to make the audience **understand and feel the pain** experienced by your customer.

---

### **Exercise**

Use the work from **Module 1 (Problem Statement)** and answer the following questions:

1️⃣ What is the core problem your customer experiences?

2️⃣ What happens when this problem occurs?

3️⃣ Why are existing solutions ineffective, frustrating, or expensive?

4️⃣ What has changed recently that makes solving this problem more important now?

This last question is often called **“Why Now?”**

Possible reasons include:

* New technologies  
* Market changes  
* New regulations  
* Changing consumer behavior  
* Cost pressures

---

### **Homework**

Write **one short paragraph** that describes:

* The customer  
* The problem they experience  
* Why the problem matters  
* Why now is the right time to solve it

Note\! \- Don’t just copy/paste your problem statement. You are telling a story now. Write down your one paragraph with that in mind.

### **The Shopify Example — Act 1: The Problem (The Villain)**

First-time Shopify store owners doing between $5k–$20k/month often manage fulfillment manually using spreadsheets, Slack messages, and disconnected shipping tools. As order volume grows, shipments get missed, customers become frustrated, and founders spend hours every week tracking down fulfillment issues instead of growing their business. Existing tools are either too fragmented or too complex for small operators, forcing founders to build fragile manual workflows just to stay afloat. This problem is becoming more urgent now because e-commerce competition and customer expectations for fast, reliable delivery have dramatically increased, while AI and workflow automation tools are finally making lightweight operational systems accessible to smaller businesses.

---

Why This Example Works

### **✅ The Customer Is Clear**

* first-time Shopify store owners  
* specific revenue range  
* understandable audience

### **✅ The Pain Is Concrete**

* missed shipments  
* frustrated customers  
* wasted founder time  
* operational chaos

### **✅ Existing Solutions Are Weak**

* spreadsheets  
* disconnected tools  
* manual workflows

### **✅ “Why Now” Is Specific**

* increased customer expectations  
* increased competition  
* AI/workflow automation becoming accessible

### **✅ It Tells a Story**

It feels like:  
👉 a real founder problem happening in the world right now

—not just a generic startup description.

---

### **A strong Act 1 should make the audience think:**

“Yes, I can clearly imagine this person struggling with this problem.”

The goal is emotional clarity and operational realism—not hype.

# Lesson 2 \- Act 2: The Solution (The Hero)

### **Goal**

Introduce your solution hypothesis as the potential answer to the problem.

At this stage you are **not describing a finished product**, but rather explaining how your idea could solve the problem.

---

### **Exercise**

Review the **Solution Hypothesis from Module 2** and answer the following questions:

1️⃣ What is the simplest way to describe your solution?

2️⃣ What outcome does this solution create for the customer?

3️⃣ What makes your approach different from current alternatives?

4️⃣ What insight or idea led you to believe this solution could work?

Focus on **value**, not features.

---

### **Homework**

Write **one paragraph** explaining:

* What your solution is  
* How it helps customers solve the problem  
* Why your approach could work better than existing solutions

This paragraph should answer:

How could this problem be solved and why might your approach work?

### **Shopify Example — Act 2: The Solution (The Hero)**

We believe small Shopify store owners don’t need another complicated enterprise operations platform—they need a lightweight workflow system that helps them stay on top of fulfillment before problems spiral out of control. Our solution automatically consolidates order status, shipping deadlines, and fulfillment tasks into a simple operational dashboard designed specifically for smaller operators managing fulfillment themselves. Instead of jumping between spreadsheets, Slack messages, and disconnected tools, founders can quickly identify delays and prevent missed shipments before customers are impacted. We believe this approach could work better than existing solutions because most current tools are either too fragmented or too complex for early-stage e-commerce businesses, while recent advances in workflow automation and AI make it possible to deliver lightweight operational coordination without adding more overhead.

---

Why This Example Works

✅ The Solution Is Simple

* lightweight workflow system  
* operational coordination  
* not overloaded with features

---

✅ The Outcome Is Clear

* fewer missed shipments  
* less operational chaos  
* founders save time  
* issues identified earlier

---

✅ It Explains Why Existing Solutions Fail

* fragmented tools  
* too much complexity  
* not designed for small operators

---

✅ The “Insight” Is Believable

The narrative explains:  
👉 why smaller Shopify operators are underserved

and:  
👉 why newer automation tools make this possible now

---

✅ It Focuses on Value, Not Features

The paragraph emphasizes:

* customer relief  
* workflow improvement  
* operational clarity

—not technical architecture.

---

**A strong Act 2 should make the audience think:**

“That actually sounds like a believable way to solve this problem.”

The goal is not to prove the startup will succeed.

The goal is to:  
👉 make the solution feel logical, grounded, and worth exploring further.

# Lesson 2 \- Pin the Pain to a Person

## **Core Exercise: *“Pin the Pain to a Person”***

This is a **structured narrowing exercise** that takes founders from vague audience → specific, reachable early adopter in a real-world moment.

### **Step 1: Broad Customer Brain Dump (5–10 minutes)**

**Prompt**

List every type of person or role that *might* experience this problem.

* Founder writes a simple bullet list. No filtering yet.

**Rules**

* Roles, not demographics (“Ops manager,” not “30–45 year olds”)  
* No “everyone,” “any company,” or “all users”

**Output**  
A rough universe of possible customers.

---

### **Step 2: Narrow to a Primary Customer Segment**

**Prompt**

From the list above, choose **one** customer type that:

* Feels the pain most frequently  
* Suffers real consequences if it’s not solved  
* Is likely aware of the problem

**Guidance**  
Ask them:

* Who complains about this today?  
* Who has budget or authority?  
* Who is already hacking together workarounds?

**Output**  
A single **primary customer segment**.

---

### **Step 3: Identify the Early Adopter Slice (Smallest Viable Market)**

**Prompt**

Now narrow that segment further to the **smallest group of people** who:

* Feel the pain most intensely  
* Are actively looking for solutions  
* Would talk to you *this month*

**Framework**  
Encourage adding **constraints**:

* Industry  
* Company size  
* Role seniority  
* Tools they already use  
* Life or business stage

**Bad Example**

“Small business owners”

**Better Example**

“First-time Shopify store owners doing $5k–$20k/month who manage fulfillment themselves”

**Output**  
A clearly constrained **early adopter slice**.

---

### **Step 4: Define the Context & Trigger Moment**

**Prompt**

Describe the **exact moment** when the problem shows up.

Have them answer:

* Where are they?  
* What are they trying to accomplish?  
* What just happened that triggered the pain?  
* What’s at stake if it goes wrong?

**Template**

“When \[customer\] is trying to \[job\] in \[context\], they struggle with \[problem\], which leads to \[negative outcome\].”

**Output**  
A concrete **scenario**, not an abstract problem.

---

## **Homework**

**1–2 sentence description** combining customer \+ context:

“We are solving \[problem\] for \[specific customer\] when they are \[specific situation or workflow\], because \[consequence if unsolved\].”

This becomes a **north star sentence** used in:

* Interview questions  
* Market research  
* MVP scoping

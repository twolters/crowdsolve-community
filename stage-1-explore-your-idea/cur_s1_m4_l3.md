# Lesson 3 \- Act 3: The Future (The Vision)

### **Goal**

Explain why this idea matters in the long term and why you are motivated to pursue it.

This part of the story helps others understand:

* Why this problem matters  
* Why the solution could grow into something meaningful  
* Why you are the person pursuing it

---

### **Exercise**

Reflect on the following questions:

1️⃣ If your idea succeeds, how will the world be different?

2️⃣ What motivates you personally to solve this problem?

3️⃣ What unique insight or experience do you bring?

4️⃣ What are the next steps needed to move this idea forward?

---

### **Homework**

Write **one paragraph** describing:

* Your vision for how this idea could impact the world  
* Why you are personally motivated to pursue this problem  
* What you plan to do next

Your paragraph should answer:

Why does this idea matter and why are you the person pursuing it?

### **Shopify Example — Act 3: The Future (The Vision)**

We believe independent e-commerce founders should be able to run reliable operations without needing a large operations team or expensive enterprise software. If this idea succeeds, small online businesses will spend less time fighting operational chaos and more time growing their companies, serving customers, and building better products. I’m personally motivated to solve this problem because I’ve experienced firsthand how quickly operational complexity overwhelms small founders once order volume starts growing, and I’ve watched talented entrepreneurs lose momentum because their systems couldn’t keep up. My background in workflow automation and operational systems gave me the insight that many of these problems are solvable with much simpler tools than what exists today. The next step is to continue interviewing Shopify founders, validate the biggest workflow bottlenecks, and build a lightweight MVP that helps operators prevent fulfillment issues before they become customer problems.

---

Why This Example Works

✅ It Explains the Bigger Vision

* empowering independent businesses  
* reducing operational chaos  
* helping founders focus on growth

The vision feels:  
👉 meaningful and believable

—not artificially world-changing.

---

✅ The Founder Motivation Feels Personal

The founder explains:

* firsthand experience  
* observed customer pain  
* emotional connection to the problem

This creates authenticity.

---

✅ It Establishes Founder Credibility

The narrative subtly communicates:

* operational understanding  
* workflow expertise  
* relevant insight

without sounding arrogant.

---

✅ The Next Steps Are Clear

The founder explains:

* customer interviews  
* workflow validation  
* MVP testing

This signals:  
👉 thoughtful execution, not just ideas.

---

✅ It Completes the Story Arc

Act 1 created tension.  
Act 2 introduced a believable solution.  
Act 3 explains:  
👉 why this matters long term and why this founder is pursuing it.

The story now feels complete.

---

### **A strong Act 3 should make the audience think:**

“This founder understands the problem deeply and has a credible reason to pursue it.”

The goal is not to sound grandiose.

The goal is to:  
👉 communicate conviction, realism, and forward momentum.

# A problem, simply stated

This module will help you articulate—crisply and evidence-based—the real problem you intend to solve, for whom, and why it matters now. This becomes the foundation for every downstream module (customer interviews, solution design, MVP, GTM).

The core elements are:

* **Who is this for?**  
* **What pain do they feel?**  
* **What causes that pain?**  
* **Can you say it clearly in one sentence?**

In this first lesson, let’s create a simple problem description. Describe, in your own words, the problem you are trying to solve. Then we will break it down and move on to the more detailed sections of your problem statement.

## **Your Homework**

1. Create a “Cohort Post” in the space reserved for your Cohort. This should have been set up before your onboarding onto the CrowdSolve platform. If you were in a prior cohort and already have this done, then you can ignore this homework. Feel free to reflect on your Refined Problem Statement however and change if appropriate. Your Post should have the following:  
   1. Name of your startup as the title to the post  
   2. Add a cover image (click on the leftmost icon at the top of your post, it should be the “set cover image” icon)  
   3. In the body of your post create the following fields  
      1. Startup Name: \[your startup name \- preferably the same name you put down when you registered\]  
         * If you didn’t register your startup name, go back into your profile and fill in.  
      2. Founders: \[All the founders names that are working with you\]  
      3. Founding Year: \[year\]  
      4. Stage: \[select one of the following: PreRevenue, Early Revenue, Scaling\]  
      5. Startup Description: \[Describe your startup in your own words\]  
2. Save your post

## **Get Marvin Feedback**

1. Save your post. Now create a comment and type “@marvin mm\_s1\_m1\_l1”  
2. Underneath that type your problem statement in your own words.  
3. Save the comment. Marvin will respond within 15 minutes with feedback. Take what you want from Marvin’s feedback and create a section below your startup description in your post and title it, “Refined Problem Statement”. Merge in whatever you’d like to take from Marvin and add your Refined Problem Statement text in your new section of your post.

# Lesson 3 — Clarify the Job to Be Done & Current Pain

**Goal**  
Clarify the **actual job customers are trying to get done**, how they handle it today, and how painful it really is—using observable behavior, not opinions.

---

## **Core Exercise: *“Describe the Last Time It Happened”***

This exercise forces founders to anchor their thinking in **real customer moments**, not hypothetical needs.

---

### **Step 1: Describe the Functional Job**

**Prompt**

What is the concrete task the customer is trying to accomplish?

Rules:

* Use verbs, not features  
* No mention of your solution  
* Avoid vague outcomes like “be more efficient”

**Examples**

* ❌ “Manage projects better”  
* ✅ “Coordinate tasks and deadlines across 5–10 contractors so work ships on time”

**Output**  
A clear **functional job statement**.

---

### **Step 2: Identify the Emotional Job**

**Prompt**

When this job is hard or fails, how does the customer *feel*?

Encourage:

* Stress  
* Anxiety  
* Fear  
* Relief  
* Confidence

**Examples**

* “Feels anxious they’ll miss a deadline”  
* “Feels embarrassed asking for status updates”

**Output**  
The emotional cost of the problem.

---

### **Step 3: Identify the Social / Identity Job**

**Prompt**

How does this problem affect how the customer wants to be seen by others—or how they see themselves?

Examples:

* “Wants to look competent to their manager”  
* “Wants to feel like a ‘real founder,’ not someone winging it”

**Output**  
The identity or social stakes tied to the job.

---

### **Step 4: Document Current Workarounds**

**Prompt**

What does the customer actually do today when this problem shows up?

Rules:

* Describe behavior, not complaints  
* Include tools, hacks, or avoidance  
* “Nothing” is rarely true—probe deeper

Ask:

* What tools do they use?  
* What manual steps do they take?  
* Who do they involve?

**Examples**

* Spreadsheet tracking  
* Slack messages  
* Manual reminders  
* Paying an agency or consultant

**Output**  
A realistic picture of **current behavior**.

---

### **Step 5: Assess Pain Severity**

**Prompt**

How painful is this problem in practice?

Have founders score and explain:

* **Frequency** – How often does this happen?  
* **Cost** – Time, money, lost revenue, risk  
* **Frustration** – Emotional or cognitive load  
* **Spend** – Do they currently pay to solve it (tools, services, people)?

**Red Flag**  
If customers don’t spend *time or money* today, pain may be weak.

**Output**  
A grounded assessment of pain intensity.

---

## **Homework (Founder Submits)**

A **short paragraph** answering:

* What job the customer is trying to accomplish  
* How they currently handle it  
* Why it’s painful (functional, emotional, social)  
* How intense the pain is today

This paragraph becomes a **truth anchor** for:

* Market research synthesis  
* MVP scoping  
* Messaging and positioning

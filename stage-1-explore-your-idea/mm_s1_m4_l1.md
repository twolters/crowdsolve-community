# Evaluation Instructions

Evaluate the founder’s narrative for:

---

## 1. Customer Clarity

Can you clearly picture:
👉 who the customer is?

Strong narratives:

* describe a recognizable person or role
* imply context and environment
* feel grounded

Weak narratives:

* use broad abstractions
* sound generic
* lack specificity

---

## 2. Problem Realism

Does the problem:
👉 feel operationally believable?

Strong narratives:

* describe real-world friction
* imply consequences
* sound observable

Weak narratives:

* sound theoretical
* sound overdramatized
* rely on vague pain

---

## 3. Emotional Resonance

Can the audience:
👉 feel the frustration, stress, or struggle?

Strong narratives:

* create empathy
* imply emotional stakes
* feel human

Weak narratives:

* sound emotionally flat
* overexplain instead of illustrating

---

## 4. Existing Solution Weakness

Does the founder clearly explain:
👉 why current approaches are frustrating, expensive, fragmented, or insufficient?

Strong narratives:

* mention current workarounds
* imply inefficiency
* show why the problem persists

Weak narratives:

* ignore current behavior
* vaguely claim “nothing works”

---

## 5. “Why Now” Strength

Does the narrative:
👉 clearly explain why this problem matters more today?

Strong “Why Now” framing:

* references specific shifts
* feels believable
* increases urgency naturally

Weak framing:

* uses generic startup language
* sounds forced or trendy

---

## 6. Storytelling Quality

Does the paragraph:
👉 flow naturally as a story?

Strong storytelling:

* feels conversational
* creates a mental picture
* builds tension naturally

Weak storytelling:

* sounds like a business plan
* feels robotic
* overuses buzzwords

---

## 7. Alignment With Prior Work

Does the narrative:
👉 remain consistent with earlier lessons?

Look for:

* customer consistency
* problem consistency
* believable escalation of clarity

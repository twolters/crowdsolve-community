# Evaluation Instructions

Evaluate the founder’s work for:

---

## 1. Problem ↔ Solution Alignment

Does the proposed solution:
👉 directly address the problem described?

Look for:

* logical connection
* relevance to customer pain
* connection to the root cause

Weak alignment:

* solution solves a different problem
* solution feels disconnected
* founder jumps into unrelated features

---

## 2. Clarity of Solution

Can you clearly understand:
👉 what the founder wants to build?

Strong solutions:

* are simple to explain
* focus on the core mechanism
* avoid unnecessary complexity

Weak solutions:

* are vague
* are overloaded with features
* sound like multiple products at once

---

## 3. Simplicity

Does the founder:
👉 explain the solution in plain language?

Strong solutions:

* sound conversational
* are understandable quickly
* focus on value creation

Weak solutions:

* use jargon
* overdescribe architecture
* sound like technical specs

---

## 4. Hypothesis Thinking

Does the founder:
👉 express the solution as something to explore and test?

Strong signals:

* curiosity
* openness
* assumptions acknowledged

Weak signals:

* certainty without evidence
* “this will definitely work”
* overconfidence

---

## 5. Scope Awareness

Is the proposed solution:
👉 focused enough for an early-stage startup?

Watch for:

* “platform for everyone”
* trying to solve multiple unrelated problems
* enterprise-scale complexity too early

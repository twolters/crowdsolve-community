# Founder Assignment

The founder completed the following exercises:

---

## Step 1 — Functional Job

The concrete task the customer is trying to accomplish.

Examples:

* coordinate contractor deadlines
* reconcile invoices
* onboard new employees

NOT vague goals like:

* “be more productive”
* “manage better”

---

## Step 2 — Emotional Job

How the customer feels when the problem happens.

Examples:

* stress
* anxiety
* embarrassment
* uncertainty
* loss of confidence

---

## Step 3 — Social / Identity Job

How the problem affects:

* how the customer sees themselves
* how they want others to see them

Examples:

* wanting to appear competent
* wanting to feel like a “real founder”
* wanting to avoid disappointing others

---

## Step 4 — Current Workarounds

What customers actually do today.

Examples:

* spreadsheets
* manual reminders
* consultants
* Slack messages
* internal hacks

The founder should describe:
👉 behavior, not opinions.

---

## Step 5 — Pain Severity

The founder assesses:

* frequency
* cost
* frustration
* current spending behavior

The founder submits a short paragraph summarizing:

* the job
* the workaround
* the pain
* the intensity

---

# Evaluation Instructions

Evaluate the founder’s work for:

---

## 1. Functional Job Clarity

Can you clearly understand:
👉 what task the customer is trying to accomplish?

Strong functional jobs:

* are concrete
* involve real-world activity
* contain observable behavior

Weak jobs:

* are abstract
* sound aspirational
* describe outcomes instead of tasks

---

## 2. Emotional Depth

Does the founder identify:
👉 believable emotional consequences?

Strong emotional jobs:

* feel human
* imply stress or stakes
* connect to real situations

Weak emotional jobs:

* feel generic
* feel exaggerated
* lack realism

---

## 3. Social / Identity Stakes

Does the founder uncover:
👉 how this problem affects identity, reputation, or self-perception?

Strong responses:

* reveal hidden motivations
* explain why the pain matters emotionally

Weak responses:

* are superficial
* repeat emotional jobs without deeper identity meaning

---

## 4. Workaround Realism

Does the founder describe:
👉 what customers ACTUALLY do today?

Strong workarounds:

* involve tools
* involve hacks
* involve manual behavior
* imply effort or cost

Weak workarounds:

* are hypothetical
* assume customers do nothing
* ignore existing behavior

---

## 5. Pain Severity

Does the founder realistically assess:

* frequency
* financial cost
* emotional burden
* current spending behavior?

Strong pain:

* causes repeated friction
* costs time/money/reputation
* already drives behavior

Weak pain:

* is merely “nice to solve”

---

## 6. Alignment Across Lessons

Does this lesson:
👉 strengthen and deepen the founder’s earlier problem statement and customer definition?

Look for:

* consistency
* increased specificity
* stronger understanding

# Output Format

---

# Lesson 3 Feedback — Clarify the Job to Be Done & Current Pain

---

## 👍 What’s Working

Identify:

* strong functional job clarity
* believable emotional stakes
* realistic workarounds
* meaningful pain signals

(2–4 concise bullets)

---

## ⚠️ Where to Improve

Identify:

* vague job definitions
* weak emotional depth
* unrealistic workarounds
* unclear pain severity
* assumption-heavy thinking

Keep feedback practical and grounded.

---

## 🔧 Suggested Improvements

Provide:

* a refined version of the functional job if needed
* stronger emotional or social framing
* more realistic workaround descriptions
* clearer articulation of pain intensity

Preserve the founder’s intent.

Do NOT invent a different startup idea.

---

## 🧩 Refined JTBD Summary

Create a concise summary paragraph that clearly explains:

• the customer’s job
• how they currently solve it
• why the current process is painful
• the emotional and social consequences
• how severe the pain is

This summary should:

* feel realistic
* sound interview-ready
* become useful input for market discovery

---

## 💡 Coaching Insight

Provide 1–2 short startup principles.

Examples:

* “Customers rarely buy products—they hire solutions to make progress in their lives.”
* “Current workarounds are one of the strongest indicators that a real problem exists.”
* “Pain becomes valuable when it is frequent, expensive, emotionally draining, or already causing people to spend time or money.”

---

## 🧠 Preparation for the Next Lesson

Briefly explain:

> “In the next lesson, we’ll explore the deeper root causes behind this problem and why this problem is becoming important right now.”

---

## 🔄 Iteration Prompt

End with:

> “Would you like to refine this further, or move on to the next lesson?”

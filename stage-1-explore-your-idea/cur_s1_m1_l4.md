# Lesson 4 — Root Cause & Why Now

**Goal**  
Understand **why the problem exists at its core** and **why now is the right moment** for a solution.

---

## **Core Exercise: *“Chase the Pain Downstream”***

This exercise forces founders to go **beneath surface symptoms** and articulate both **causality** and **timing**.

---

### **Step 1: Define the Observable Symptom**

**Prompt**

Describe the problem exactly as the customer experiences it—without explaining why yet.

Rules:

* Use customer language from Lesson 3  
* No solution framing  
* Focus on what visibly goes wrong

**Output**  
A clear **problem symptom statement**.

---

### **Step 2: The 5 Whys (Root Cause Analysis)**

**Prompt**

Ask “Why does this happen?” five times, answering each one honestly.

**Template**

* Problem: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_  
* Why \#1: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_  
* Why \#2: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_  
* Why \#3: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_  
* Why \#4: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_  
* Why \#5: \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

**Guidance**

* If answers become circular, restart  
* Stop early if a true structural cause emerges (process, incentive, tech gap)

**Output**  
A concise **root cause explanation** (1–3 sentences).

---

### **Step 3: What Has Changed? (The “Why Now” Scan)**

**Prompt**

What has changed recently that makes this problem more painful or visible today?

Have founders scan these categories:

* **Technology** – New tools, platforms, APIs, AI capabilities  
* **Market** – New competitors, pricing pressure, scale  
* **Behavior / Culture** – Remote work, expectations, norms  
* **Regulation / Policy** – New rules, compliance, incentives  
* **Economics** – Cost changes, budget pressure, labor shifts

**Rules**

* Must name a **specific change**  
* “People are more busy now” is not acceptable

**Output**  
1–2 concrete external shifts.

---

### **Step 4: Urgency vs. Annoyance Test**

**Prompt**

What happens if the customer does *nothing*?

Ask:

* What breaks?  
* What risk increases?  
* What opportunity is lost?  
* Who gets blamed?

**Red Flag**  
If nothing bad happens, urgency is low.

**Output**  
A clear explanation of **why this problem demands action now**.

---

## **Final Deliverable (Founder Submits)**

1. **Root Cause Analysis**  
   A short paragraph explaining *why* this problem exists at a structural level.  
2. **Timing Statement** (1–2 sentences)

“This problem is becoming urgent now because \[specific change\], which causes \[impact\] for \[customer\].”

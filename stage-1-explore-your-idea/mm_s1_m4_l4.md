# Evaluation Instructions

Evaluate the founder’s story for:

---

## 1. Narrative Clarity

Can you clearly understand:
👉 the startup story from beginning to end?

Strong narratives:

* flow naturally
* explain customer pain clearly
* explain the solution logically
* end with believable vision and momentum

Weak narratives:

* feel disconnected
* overcomplicate the solution
* lack emotional continuity

---

## 2. Pitch Readiness

Does the story:
👉 translate naturally into a slide-based format?

Strong stories:

* contain clear sections
* communicate key ideas quickly
* imply strong slide structure naturally

Weak stories:

* ramble
* repeat ideas
* lack focus

---

## 3. Problem / Solution Flow

Does the transition:
👉 from Act 1 → Act 2 feel natural?

Strong transitions:

* create tension
* clearly resolve tension
* explain why the solution matters

Weak transitions:

* feel abrupt
* lose the customer context

---

## 4. Founder Credibility

Does the story:
👉 create confidence in the founder’s thinking?

Strong credibility:

* feels grounded
* acknowledges uncertainty appropriately
* communicates thoughtful momentum

Weak credibility:

* overhypes
* overpromises
* sounds unrealistic

---

## 5. Simplicity

Can someone:
👉 understand the startup quickly?

Strong decks:

* simplify complexity
* avoid jargon
* communicate one idea at a time

Weak decks:

* overload slides mentally
* sound too technical
* introduce too many concepts

# Suggested Deck Structure

Use this structure as a starting point, but adapt if the founder’s story suggests a better flow.

---

## Slide 1 — Title

### Purpose

Introduce the startup clearly.

### Suggested Content

* startup name
* one-line description
* founder name(s)

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem

### Purpose

Explain the customer pain.

### Suggested Content

* operational struggle
* customer frustration
* core pain point

---

## Slide 3 — Why This Matters

### Purpose

Explain the consequences of the problem.

### Suggested Content

* lost time
* lost revenue
* operational chaos
* emotional impact

---

## Slide 4 — Who Experiences This Problem

### Purpose

Define the customer segment.

### Suggested Content

* target customer
* workflow context
* early adopter profile

---

## Slide 5 — Current Solutions

### Purpose

Show why existing approaches fail.

### Suggested Content

* spreadsheets
* manual workarounds
* fragmented tools
* operational friction

---

## Slide 6 — The Insight

### Purpose

Explain the realization behind the idea.

### Suggested Content

* market shift
* behavioral insight
* “Why Now”
* founder observation

---

## Slide 7 — The Solution

### Purpose

Introduce the solution hypothesis.

### Suggested Content

* core mechanism
* customer workflow improvement
* key value creation

---

## Slide 8 — How It Works

### Purpose

Explain the concept at a high level.

### Suggested Content

* lightweight workflow
* process simplification
* operational coordination

Avoid:

* technical architecture overload

---

## Slide 9 — The Vision

### Purpose

Describe the broader future impact.

### Suggested Content

* customer transformation
* market change
* long-term potential

---

## Slide 10 — Why This Team

### Purpose

Explain founder motivation and credibility.

### Suggested Content

* founder insight
* personal motivation
* relevant experience

---

## Slide 11 — What We Are Doing Next

### Purpose

Show momentum and learning plan.

### Suggested Content

* customer interviews
* MVP testing
* validation plan
* next milestones

---

## Slide 12 — Call for Help

### Purpose

Clearly explain what the founder is seeking.

### Suggested Content

* introductions
* customer conversations
* mentors
* technical help
* cofounders
* feedback
